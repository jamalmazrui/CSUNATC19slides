2017/2018 Pearson User Studies

Ø   Findings:

Ø   Not all blind students were as comfortable with technology as they were with embossed braille.

Ø   Refreshable braille devices can only show one line of content at a time and line length varies, based on the type of device used. For more complex math, or math that requires a certain structure, students indicated that it is much easier to understand the context as a whole when the math is presented in braille on paper.

Additional Findings

Ø Online math relies upon alternative text rather than single numerals and operators. So even something simple like “(3+y)*10=6” becomes “open parenthesis 3 plus y close parenthesis times 10 equals 6.” If a student using refreshable braille and/or a screen reader wants to review that equation, they cannot navigate numeral by numeral or operator by operator because the math equation will be expressed in words. When equations are embossed, a blind student can read them the same way a visual user would. Unfortunately, embossed expressions are not included in tactile graphics supplements unless they happen to be part of a larger graphic.
