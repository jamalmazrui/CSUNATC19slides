Framework

Supporting dimensions of accessibility

1 Culture and Attitudes (CA)

§   Foster a culture of diversity that includes people with disabilities and position digital accessibility as an organizational priority.

-         Business Culture: Alignment of accessibility with business culture

-         Core Values: Alignment of accessibility with core values

-         Engaged Community: Digital accessibility as a shared concern and responsibility

2 Accessibility Strategy (AS)

§   Define the boundaries of the accessibility program and establish foundational principles and structures to guide program activities.

-         Defined Governance: Defined oversight for accessibility activities

-         Digital Strategy: Alignment of accessibility with digital strategy

3 Program Development (PD)

§   Provide program support, with defined and prioritized program activities, and ongoing program assessment.

-         Program Services: Accessibility services and support

-         Fixing and Monitoring: Improving accessibility in digital resources

4 Processes and Procedures (PP)

§   Identify relevant processes and procedures across the organization and improve their support for accessibility.

-         Accessible Procurement: Accessibility in procurement processes

-         Accessible Development: Digital accessibility in digital resource development

5 Knowledge and Skills (KS)

§   Integrate accessibility into organizational outreach and workforce development and strengthen in-house expertise.

-         Training Programs: Training programs focused on accessibility

-         Workforce Development: Accessibility in workforce development

-         Community of Practice: Accessibility in communities of practice

6 Resources and Infrastructure (RI)

§   Build an infrastructure of tools and resources to support best practices in accessibility.

-         Instructions: Guidance on accessibility best practices

-         Systems: Accessibility support in digital content creation tools

7 Support and Communication (SC)

§   Demonstrate ongoing commitment and progress and provide help and support.

-         Dialog: Communication strategy for digital accessibility

-         Assistance: Technology support for people with disabilities

Methodology

Mapping evidence to the framework

Current State Analysis Activities

§   Documentation Review: Documents the describe how digital resources are produced, provided, and supported

§   Stakeholder Interviews: People who make decisions about digital resources and people affected by those decisions

§   Visioning Workshop: People who are currently responsible for supporting accessibility

Observations and Evidence

§   Assets and Opportunities: Resources and activities that currently support accessibility—or that could

§   Challenges and Barriers: Aspects of the environment that make accessibility progress difficult or impossible

§   What’s Needed: Perspectives on what would make the accessibility program more successful

Accessibility is integrated into culture and practice, such that everyone knows their role and has the skills and resources they need to be successful.

1 Culture and Attitudes

§   Engaged Community — Digital accessibility as a shared concern and responsibility

-         Shared commitment — Ensure everyone who influences the digital environment understands their role in making it accessible.

§   Business Culture — Alignment of accessibility with business culture

-         Recognized requirements for accessibility — Include non-discrimination requirements in business strategy and commit to meeting accessibility requirements.

Do you include accessibility responsibilities in technology-related position descriptions, such as front-end developer, content editor, and video producer?

Do you have a high-level articulation of your organization’s accessibility commitment?

5 Knowledge and Skills

§   Training Programs — Training programs focused on accessibility

-         Dedicated training programs — Provide accessibility training to as many staff as possible to help create a baseline understanding of key concepts and specialized training relevant to specific roles.

§   Workforce Development — Accessibility in workforce development

-         Accessibility as a valued professional skill—Identify opportunities to integrate accessibility knowledge into professional development.

Do you offer role-based accessibility training programs to build skills? Do you require staff to refresh their accessibility awareness and skills?

Do you encourage staff to attend events on accessibility-related topics, to build their accessibility awareness and skills?
