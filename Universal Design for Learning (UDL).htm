Implementing New EdTech: Engaging Students with Audio Note Taking Software

Friday @ 11:00am in Grand JK

In Today’s Session

This session is for anyone seeking resources available for instructors to use in the classroom and can be adopted as part of a wider commitment to Universal Design for Learning (UDL).

We will present an instructor's approach to providing lectures for diverse learners, integrating Audio Notetaker,  PowerPoint and transcription software, to help engage students in their learning process.

We’ll discuss what we have learned about this process over the past several years as well as how students can use key features of Sonocent note taking products to help support their educational goals.

Step 1: Capture

1.     Open Sonocent Audio Notetaker and set to capture the lecture

2.     Start digital recorder

a.   Allows for instructor to move around (untethered)

b.   For better audio quality (audio replace)

3.     Start  PowerPoint Lecture

4.     When lecture is finished, go back to Audio Notetaker and click finish

“Make sure to save!!!”

Step 2: Produce

1.     Replace Audio

–   Combine 2 audio files into 1 (computer/digital recorder)

–   Program will automatically choose highest quality recording

2.     Transcribe

▪ How much time does it take?

–    Nibity has a 24 hour (per 1 hour lecture)  turnaround time
