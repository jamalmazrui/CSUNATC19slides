Role-based Challenges of Implementing Accessibility Remediation

Today’s Goals

•    Get a better understanding of how accessibility is perceived by different roles under various constraints.

•    Understand how those “downhill” from an accessibility review might perceive our reports.

Scenario: Inheriting someone else’s mistakes

Background: Custom e-commerce application built by a large 3rd party service provider over several years.

 

Primary Driver: Lawsuit filed under Title III of the ADA

 

Issue found: A blind visitor to the site is unable to purchase an item from the application.

 

Limitation: Application is now maintained by a small group of in-house developers.

Scenario: Buried by content

Background: Educator with several hundred online courses utilizing various formats including: Web, PDF and Video formats.

 

Primary Driver: A student has filed a complaint that course materials are not available to them.

 

Issue Found: Videos lack captions and audio descriptions

 

Limitation: Individual departments maintain their own IT staff, but instructors are responsible for course content.

Scenario: Don’t know what we don’t know…

Background: Company wants to rebuild an application using ReactJS and leverage it to create one UI that can work across desktop and mobile devices.

 

Primary Driver: Market and legal forces are pushing the team to think carefully about accessibility.

 

Issue Found: No one knows where to start.

 

Limitation: No one on the team knows anything about accessibility, but they all have WAVE and Axe installed.

Scenario: I can’t touch this

Background: Startup that has developed a native mobile application for a new method of tracking diet and fitness.

 

Primary Driver: A new investor has a family member that lacks the dexterity to perform complex interactions on a mobile device.

 

Issue Found: Mobile application utilizes various multi-point gestures for interacting and completing tasks within the application.

 

Limitation: Product roadmap has already been defined and accessibility isn’t on it.

Scenario: “Danger, Will Robinson!”©

Background: Telecomm company is developing a new interface for its online programming.

 

Primary Driver: Compliance with the 21st Century Communications and Video Accessibility Act (CVAA) or risk fines

 

Issue Found: Internal review has found that emergency notifications are not accessible in the new interface

 

Limitation: Initial launch is in one month
