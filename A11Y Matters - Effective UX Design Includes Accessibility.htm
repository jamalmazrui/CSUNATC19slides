Appearance is Not a Criteria

•    Thousands shared photos of themselves on Twitter with the hash tag, “#InvisiblyDisabledLooksLike”.

•    The continuing stigma of disability likely prevented far more from tweeting.

What does this mean?

•    You can’t make assumptions about people’s ability based upon appearance.

•    And you can’t expect people to divulge that information.

•    Ability exists on a continuum.

The Solution

“Shifting Left” means that teams must continually communicate and coordinate

•    Siloed teams are deadly.

•    Accessibility requirements should be in functional requirements for every component and included in acceptance criteria.

•    Whatever interaction behavior is not present in the wireframe must be defined as interaction specifications.

•    Design patterns must include accessibility acceptance criteria.

•    Testing must include accessibility testing – and more than just a quick cursory test.

•    Content and design must be stable at the beginning and coordinated with Design and Development (example).
