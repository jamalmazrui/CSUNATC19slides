Heather Solberg, CVRT Victoria Wheeler, COMS, CVRT, CATIS

Objectives

At the conclusion of this educational program, learners will be able to:

1.                 Describe accessibility features available on Apple TV, Fire TV, and Roku.

2.                 Compare prices of Apple TV, Fire TV, and Roku.

3.                 Identify how to enable accessibility features during setup for Apple TV, Fire TV, and Roku.

4.                 Discuss pros/cons of accessibility features for Apple TV, Fire TV, and Roku.

Text to Speech (VoiceOver)

•    Turn on/off by triple-clicking the Menu button

•    Will work for setup of Apple TV

•    Does not automatically read what is on the screen

•    Ensure rotor is set to “read screen after delay”

•    Not intuitive to navigate

•    Use Rotor Gestures

•    Direct Touch = scrolling, skips over text

•    Vertical navigation = flick left/right = similar to “normal” VoiceOver gestures; need to use Rotor and change option to move up/down 

•    Follow Focus = flick up / down / left / right to move throughout page

•    Ability to make changes in Settings (similar to on iOS devices)

Braille Displays

•    Compatible with over 70 refreshable braille displays

Zoom

•   Turn on/off by clicking trackpad 3 times

•   Gestures are not intuitive and different than other iOS devices

•   Trackpad size can be a challenge

•   Can Zoom on video content, but can’t pan screen while video is playing

•   Panning: (2 finger tap)

•   Move with 1 finger around the screen

•   Use 2 fingers to change selection in menu

•   Panning moves too quickly, especially with high magnification

•   Follow Focus (2 finger tap)

•   Move up/down menu with 1 finger flick up/down

Display Accommodations, etc

•   Turn on/off in Settings

•   Bold text doesn’t make much of a difference

•   Appearance = light, dark, or automatic

•   Display = color filters, light sensitivity, reduce white point

Other Settings

•   Audio Description – ability to turn on in settings and it will work in apps

•   Slow down movement (doesn’t work well)

•   Settings > Remotes & Devices> Touch Surface Tracking > Slow

•   Home Button

•   Can be changed to either Apple TV app or Home Screen

Fire TV

Text to Speech (VoiceView)

•   Turn on/off by holding down the Back and Menu buttons on Fire TV remote at same time for two seconds.

•   “VoiceView Ready” will be heard

•   A tutorial comes on the first time you turn it on (this can be skipped)

•   Can use during set up

•   Some VoiceView settings include:

•   Adjust speech rate and volume

•   Choose Key Echo and Punctuation feedback

•   Review mode (Tutorial) gives opportunity to explore keys at anytime

•   Access through settings > accessibility > VoiceView > VoiceView Tutorial • Works with normal use of remote

Screen Magnifier

•   Enable in Settings > Accessibility

•   Turn on/off Screen Magnifier by holding Back and Fast Forward

•   Zoom in/out by holding Menu and FFW or RW

•   Point of reference in the top right corner of the screen during panning

•   Smooth panning

•   Difficult with dexterity issues

***Can zoom on video content, but can’t pan screen while video is playing High Contrast Text (Expirmental)

•   Only one option, white lettering with black outline (didn’t see much difference)

Smart Speaker Compatibility

•   Apple HomePod – Not compatible 

•   Google Home – Not compatible

•   Amazon Echo – Yes

•   Must be connected to same Wi-Fi network

•   Turn on speaker capability through Alexa app

•   See Alexa app > Settings > TV and Video > Follow directions to set up content and service providers

•   Can search content / launch channels / control playback

Text to Speech (Audio Guide)

•   Turn on by pressing * key below directional keypad on the bottom right of the remote 4 times quickly

•   Settings > Accessibility

•   Adjust speech rate

•   Adjust text to speech volume

•   Disable the remote shortcut

•   Cannot change voice

•   Works with normal use of remote

•   Does not automatically speak result    when using voice search on remote

Remote

•Only tactile buttons provide ease of use

•Shortcut buttons to most popular apps

•Press and hold microphone button to dictate

• Able to find apps to download with dictation

•Can control TV power/volume

Roku Mobile App - iOS

•   Keyboard search (no voice search)

•   Private listening with headphones

•   Up to 3 additional devices

•   Pop up ads can impede use

•   Can be removed for $1.99

•   Buttons are not labeled correctly for

VoiceOver

•   the arrow keys are labeled 8 (up), 5,

4, 6 (middle row) and 7 (down)

•   Trackpad is not available when using VoiceOver

Roku Mobile App - Android

•Very similar layout to the hardware remote

•Voice/Keyboard search

•   Voice search not always accurate “I did not get that”

•   Can direct you to the mobile app on your device, rather than control Roku

•   Voice Search does not work well with TalkBack

•   Voice Search will type what TalkBack says

•TalkBack works with most of the buttons, but won’t say anything on the middle arrow keys and OK button

Misc.:

•   Easy to add/delete/move apps

•   Menu circles back

•   Featured Free option

•   Easy to access programming options in playback (ie: Audio descriptions in Netflix)

•   Pause content, then follow prompts

•   Cast/mirror photos, videos, and music from mobile device to TV

•   Use app of content to cast

•   Might not work for all apps (ie: Hulu would not cast)

•   No Direct Play: “Play the Last Kingdom on Netflix” will provide all the options for the Last Kingdom rather than opening Netflix to play this program

•   Roku can be provided to Spectrum customers for accessibility

Smart Speaker Compatibility

•Apple HomePod - not compatible

•Google Home – yes, with some limitations (USA only)

•   Must be connected to same Wi-Fi network

•   Connect devices through Google Home app

•   Can search content / launch channels / control playback

•   Netflix is not supported

•Amazon Echo – yes (as of 3/5/19), with some limitations (USA only)

•   Must be connected to same Wi-Fi network

•   Enable Roku skill in the Alexa app, then connect devices by following prompts

•   Can search content / launch channels / control playback

•   Netflix not supported

Pros

•Audio Guide is easy to use

•Tactile buttons on remote

•Quick channel buttons on remote

•Easy to add/delete channels

•Large/simple visual display of

channels

•Menu cycles through options

•Can be controlled with Google Home & Amazon Echo

Cons

•   iOS app not useful due to ads

•   Mobile apps are not fully accessible with text to speech

•   Different on screen keyboard for setup and channel logins (A-Z vs QWERTY)

•   Unable to change Audio Guide voice

•   Audio Guide does not automatically  activate for voice search results

•   No Audio Description setting

•   No direct play option

•   No instruction for use of accessibility features during setup

Accessibility Overview

•Text to Speech is easier to use on all platforms than magnification

•   Only accessibility option available during setup across all platforms

•   No instructions provided in print materials, or upon installing devices, on how to enable during setup

•Mobile apps are not advisable for any platform if using text to speech

(with the possible exception of Apple TV on iOS)

•Voice remotes work well for finding content on all platforms

•Remotes with tactile buttons were easier to use than a trackpad

•Smart speakers can provide some voice control of Roku & Fire TV, very limited control on Apple TV
