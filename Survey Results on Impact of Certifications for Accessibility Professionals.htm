Denis Boudreau, CPWA

Denis Boudreau is an internationally-recognized Web accessibility speaker, consultant and trainer. An expert member of the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) in the Education and Outreach Working Group (EOWG) and a Certified Professional in Web Accessibility (CPWA), he has been advocating inclusion for people with disabilities and the elderly on the Web for over 19 years. Denis is also a member of the Canadian Association of Professional Speakers (CAPS), and he currently acts as the Montreal Chapter's 2019 Communications Chair and President-Elect for 2020. A shameless geek with a fixation on inclusive design, Denis as a passion for interfaces that emotionally resonate, are beautifully engaging and delightfully accessible.

Katie Haritos-Shea, CPWA

Katie Haritos-Shea joined EVERFI in September 2018. She is responsible for providing executive vision to drive and influence Accessibility improvements in EVERFI’s Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) subscriptions, products and digital learning services. She helps to identify Accessibility barriers and provide remediation guidance in digital content. Before joining EVERFI, Katie was Principal ICT Accessibility Architect for Deque. Katie is a subject matter expert in US Section 508 and international IT/ICT accessibility standards. Katie has been actively involved in W3C technical standards working groups (WCAG, Web Payments, Privacy, APA, Web Platform, etc.) as an Invited Expert since 2000. She was appointed Chair of the W3C Web Accessibility Initiative’s Interest Group (WAI IG) by Tim Berners Lee in 2015. Katie donates her time on the Board of Directors for an Accessibility non-profit, Knowbility, and is on the W3C’s Advisory Committee as Knowbility’s representative.

 

Thomas Logan, CPWA

Thomas Logan has spent the past sixteen years assisting organizations to create technology solutions that work for people with disabilities. He is the owner of Equal Entry, whose mission is to: “contribute to a more accessible world.” His consulting work has spanned project deliverables for numerous federal, state, and local government agencies as well as private organizations from startups to Fortune 500s. He is co-organizer of Accessibility New York City, a monthly Meetup for the local community interested in topics related to accessibility and people with disabilities.

Samantha Evans, MBA

Sam Evans manages the IAAP Certification Initiatives. Sam’s marketing, non-profit, and certification background brings knowledge and expertise to develop, grow, and implement  professional certification programs. She brings hands-on experience in certification program evaluation, processes, item-writing, item-reviewing, and administration to the IAAP Certification Initiative. She oversees platform testing and delivery, manages test partners, and both staff and volunteer contributors to the IAAP Certification Initiatives. Sam began her career leading international membership associations for 10 years before offering strategic consulting services to non-profit organizations. Sam has spent her professional career helping non-profit organizations and educational institutions develop success strategies, tell their distinctive stories, reach new markets, and build long-lasting relationships.

Promotions and New Jobs from CPACC

5 respondents said they have been promoted or obtained a new job they credit to their CPACC credential.

 

•     By earning the CPACC credential I proved to myself and others that I am a valued resource for questions regarding accessibility. I have been promoted internally and have received several contacts from recruiters for positions outside of the company since earning the CPACC certification.

•     My customers are positive and responsive with regard to my certification.  My role at the company is business development and when they see the certification they are pleased at the level of my knowledge.  They don't expect it from "sales".  I have encouraged MANY of my customers to get the certification to benefit them at their companies, universities or agencies.

•     We now begin projects with user-centered design at the forefront. CPACC and accessibility advances this approach. As we take on more projects, colleagues ask me how we can make this as accessible as possible.

•     I believe that the CPACC is still relatively new, but is gaining momentum as a recognized certification, and in the near future will be much more widely promoted.

Promotions and New Jobs from WAS

3 respondents said they have been promoted or obtained a new job that they credit to their WAS credential.

 

•     I promote WAS more actively outside the industry because I think companies desperately need a way to engage qualified developers.

•     I will be recommending WAS as a requirement for recruitment/procurement (vendors).

•     The WAS certification has given me credibility in my organization as an accessibility leader.

•     I ended up getting a higher paying job on an accessibility team after the certification. I think the WAS is likely the thing that got me the interview.
